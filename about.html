Key points about treasure hunts:

Clues: The core element of a treasure hunt is the clues, which are strategically placed and can include riddles, cryptic messages, maps, or even physical objects that need to be deciphered to find the next location.

Competition: Treasure hunts can be played individually or in teams, with the first to find the treasure usually declared the winner.

Variety of settings: Treasure hunts can be held indoors, outdoors, in a specific area like a park, or even across a whole city, depending on the complexity of the clues.

Adaptability: The difficulty of a treasure hunt can be adjusted to suit different age groups and skill levels by adjusting the complexity of the clues.

How to play a treasure hunt

  1. Plan where to hide the treasure
  2. Decide on a series of clues
  3. Draw pictures for each clue
  4. Hide the treasure and clues
  5. Give the first clue to the players
  6. Players follow the clues to find the treasure
